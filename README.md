# Project 7 – Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) vs Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Analysis

## Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing the relationship between Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) and Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC). The goal of this project is to evaluate the efficiency of customer acquisition strategies and determine whether the revenue generated from customers justifies the cost spent to acquire them.

The analysis helps businesses understand whether their marketing and acquisition investments are profitable and sustainable.

## Tools Used
- Tableau
- Microsoft Excel
- Data Visualization
- Data Analysis

## Workflow
1. Imported the dataset into Tableau.
2. Cleaned and structured the data for analysis.
3. Calculated Customer Lifetime Value (LTV).
4. Calculated Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC).
5. Built visualizations to compare LTV and CAC performance.
6. Designed a dashboard to clearly present insights and trends.

## Key Insights
- The LTV:CAC ratio helps evaluate the profitability of customer acquisition.
- When LTV is significantly higher than CAC, the business is acquiring customers efficiently.
- Monitoring this ratio helps businesses optimize marketing and acquisition spending.
- Maintaining a healthy LTV:CAC ratio ensures long-term business growth and sustainability.

## Conclusion
The analysis highlights the importance of balancing customer acquisition costs with the long-term value generated by customers. By tracking the LTV:CAC ratio, businesses can make informed decisions about marketing strategies and improve overall profitability.
